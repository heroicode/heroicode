Things I no longer do

23 May 2017

There are a number of things I used to do as a programmer that I have stopped doing. Some of those things are worth talking about.

I no longer use Eclipse. I never liked Eclipse. I always disliked its way of modeling projects. I didn’t like how it integrated with important external tools, such as Maven. The somewhat arbitrary design choices that went into “perspectives” – which were really just fancy edit modes, a very old and not very good idea invented when punch cards were still a thing – made many tasks more difficult than necessary. The lack of support for themes and color schemes, frankly, still shocks me, and the fact that they couldn’t bother to get that right even when they decided to rebuild the entire IDE for version 4 indicates the ingrained level of misanthropy and misguidedness that plagues the project. Absent very particular requirements, I will never go back to Eclipse. Several places I worked at mandated the use of Eclipse, and I refuse to work for an organization with such a misguided policy.
